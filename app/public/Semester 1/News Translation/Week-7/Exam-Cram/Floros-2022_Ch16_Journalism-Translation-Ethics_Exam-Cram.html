2. The Asymmetrical Relationship Problem

Why Journalists Underestimate Translation (4 Reasons):

  1. Power dynamics: Translation done by journalists, not translators (Bielsa 2007: 135)
  2. Professional hierarchy: Journalism = "fourth estate" vs translation's lower status
  3. Outdated conception: Journalists see translation as "word-for-wordism"
  4. Lack of consensus: No stable TS definition to disseminate
‚ö° EXAM TIP: Darwish (2009) surveyed 370+ global media ethics codes - NONE mention translation as factor in ensuring accuracy/objectivity. This demonstrates journalism's neglect of translation ethics.

5. Kohlberg's Theory of Moral Development (1969)

‚ö° HIGH-PROBABILITY EXAM TOPIC: Kohlberg's 3 stages applied to journalism/translation ethics
Stage Characteristics Corresponds To
1. Preconventional Personal interests; extrinsic motivations; avoid punishment Consequentialist ethics; Risk management
2. Conventional Desire to "be good"; maintain social norms Professional codes; Social responsibility (partial)
3. Postconventional Highest moral conscience; critical/independent thinking; Kantian universality Virtue ethics; Social responsibility (full)

6. Social Norms Research (Lee, Coleman & Molyneux 2016)

Injunctive Norms: Perception of what others think we SHOULD do
‚Üí If journalists believe others approve unethical behavior ‚Üí they act unethically
Descriptive Norms: Perception of what others ARE ACTUALLY doing
‚Üí If journalists believe everyone else acts ethically ‚Üí they act ethically
‚ö° IMPLICATION FOR TRANSLATION: Translators work in isolation (unlike journalists in newsrooms), so professional organizations must promote beneficial descriptive norms to foster ethical behavior.

9. Synergy Framework - Concessions Needed

Translation Studies Must:

  1. Develop TOPC framework - translation in other professional contexts
  2. Offer theoretical apparatus - Skopos theory, translational action, polysystems
  3. Expand to translation policies - ethical perspective on gatekeeping/newsworthiness

Journalism Studies Must:

  1. Recognize translation sophistication - beyond correspondence retrieval
  2. See translation as resembling journalism - in mediation framework
  3. Include in ethics codes - systematic attention to translation
