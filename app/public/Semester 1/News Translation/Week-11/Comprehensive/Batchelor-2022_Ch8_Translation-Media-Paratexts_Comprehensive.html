1. Introduction: Origins of Paratext Theory

G√©rard Genette's Paratext (1980s)

Core Innovation: Literary work = TEXT + PARATEXT

Key Insight: Paratexts play crucial role in determining how text is received

Broad Definition: What "enables a text to become a book" - author's name, title, preface, illustrations, binding, typesetting

Genette's Typology

Type Definition Examples
Peritext Attached to the text Title, preface, illustrations, binding, typesetting
Epitext External to the text Author interviews, reviews, author's gender

Definition Type: Functional + Material

Key Constraint: Connection to authorial intention/responsibility

2. Jonathan Gray's Adaptation to Audiovisual Media (2010)

Gray's "Show Sold Separately" (2010)

Central Argument: "Decisions on what to watch, what not to watch, and how to watch are often made while consuming hype, synergy, and promos, so that by the time we actually encounter 'the show itself,' we have already begun to decode it and to preview its meanings and effects."

Gray's Indicative List of Media Paratexts

  • Ads, previews, trailers
  • Interviews with creative personnel
  • Internet discussion
  • Entertainment news, reviews
  • Merchandising
  • Guerrilla marketing campaigns
  • Fan creations
  • Posters, games, DVDs, CDs, spinoffs

6. Theoretical Issues

Question 1: Is the Concept of Paratext Needed?

Two aspects:

  1. Is it a useful conceptual tool?
  2. Does it open up original perspectives or entire areas of enquiry?

Question 2: How to Define Paratext for Translation Media Research?

The Boundary Problem

Genette: Physical + functional definition, constrained by authorial intention

Media/Digital Studies: Functional definition, broad range of producers

Risk (Nottingham-Martin 2014): "Where is the line between paratext and context?"

Danger (Rockenberger 2014): Paratext risks "collapsing into the vastness of 'the context'"

Proposed Solutions

Solution 1: Batchelor's (2018) Definition

Paratext = "A consciously crafted threshold for a text which has the potential to influence the way(s) in which the text is received"

Advantage: Maintains distinction between broader context/happenstance and paratext

Problem: Thorny difficulties in application

Solution 2: Wittgensteinian Approach

Model: Define by examples rather than boundaries

Method: "This and other similar things are paratexts"

Advantage: Case-by-case basis rather than abstract general terms

Support: Genette himself argued "however indeterminable its boundaries, the paratext retains at its centre a distinctive and undisputed territory"

Why Use the Term "Paratext"?

Benefit 1: Validation of Research Area

Jonathan Gray (2015): "We need the word as a reminder ‚Äì an insistence, even ‚Äì to look at paratexts. ... it's all too easy to fall back on a mode of scholarship that is centred and isolated on the work itself"

Function: Grouping ancillary materials under umbrella term validates research into apparently secondary/ephemeral materials

Result: Encourages noticing potentially valuable objects of study that might otherwise be overlooked

Benefit 2: Interdisciplinary Dialogue

Advantage: Adopting term with rich body of research in neighbouring disciplines (media/digital studies) facilitates deeper dialogue

Foundation: Theoretical underpinnings rigorously debated and developed (especially by Gray)

Adaptability: Can be further adapted to suit material at hand

7. Methodological Issues

Challenge 1: Variability in Paratextual Experience

Multiple Entry Points

Digital paratexts: Viewers may access audiovisual material through:

  • Official website
  • Fan-created video
  • Online review by another viewer/gamer

News stories: Accessible through:

  • Twitter
  • Embedded links in other articles
  • Online newspaper's home page

Personalization of On-Demand Services

Gray on Netflix: "We're all getting potentially really different experiences of texts and of the textual world based on what the various algorithms around us think they know about us. On my Apple TV, for instance, when I log into Netflix, there is no category or menu of Netflix Originals. [...] the flow that Netflix gives me is not the flow that Netflix gives you or someone else."

Methodological Adaptations for Variability

Adaptation 1: Hypothetical Temporal Model

Nottingham-Martin (2014): Rather than fixed temporal categories, ask "what if" reader experiences paratextual element before/simultaneously with/after encountering anchor-text

Adaptation 2: "Loud" vs "Quiet" Paratexts

Distinction: Between paratexts that cannot be avoided vs. those less likely to be accessed

Method: Incorporate study of flows to digital text sites (Stewart 2010) to ascertain which paratexts (more/most) viewers actually access

Challenge 2: Ephemerality

Two Types of Ephemerality

  1. Short Duration: Channel idents (15-25 seconds) - BUT watched thousands of times (total viewing time significant)
  2. Short Availability: Bus shelter posters (week or two per film)

Consequences for Scholarship

Gray (2016) on Mad Men:

  • Many academic articles focus on it as feminist text
  • BUT many paratexts currently surrounding show are "reducing" or "outright destroying" feminism by presenting actresses as objects of sexual desire
  • Problem: "By and large, [...] most libraries hold on to the product itself, not the paratexts"
  • Result: Future analysts trying to understand what Mad Men "was and what it did in social, cultural terms" won't have access to all relevant material
  • Conclusion: Reliability of their analyses - and current efforts to analyse media products from the past - must be cast into doubt

Solutions to Ephemerality Challenge

Archiving Strategies

  • Research methods from internet studies (Br√ºgger 2011)
  • Applicable to web-based paratexts (industry- or viewer-created)
  • BUT challenges of constantly mutating material remain significant

Physical Locatedness Challenge

  • Industry-created paratexts (adverts on buses, public places, TV ad breaks) geographically specific
  • Unlike audiovisual products (available recorded/can be requested), paratexts usually fleeting, not preserved
  • Researcher may not even be aware they exist
  • Solution: Most feasible for researchers based in target culture OR with dedicated "fieldwork" time (akin to observational research)

8. Future Directions

Potential Benefits for AVT Research

  1. Promotional/Ancillary Materials: Increased focus on materials accompanying imported audiovisual products ‚Üí fuller accounts of audience perception
  2. Holistic Approach: Draw fan-created, viewer-created, industry-created thresholds under one umbrella (rather than narrow rubrics like "fan-subbing")
  3. Agency Questions: Greater attention to networks involved in importing audiovisual products + power dynamics between individuals/groups
  4. Economic Factors: Connection between investment levels, quantity/quality of paratextual information, and success of imported films/shows

Potential Benefits for News Translation Research

Less clear than AVT (limited take-up in communication studies too)

  • Multiple Thresholds: Online news accessed via wide range, all influencing reader decisions AND interpretations
  • Uninterrogated Power: Many thresholds haven't drawn sustained attention - power of elements and agents producing them relatively unexplored
  • Holistic Approach: Umbrella term (rather than solely "headlines" or "leads") for considering reader/viewer experience holistically
  • Agency/Economics: Already considerable concern, but might be brought even further to fore

Accessibility: "Accessible Paratext" (M√©vel 2020)

Innovation

Foundation for new production practice rather than designating existing activity

Point of Departure

Many d/Deaf audiences perceive theatre as "not for them/not in their language" (Wilmington 2017)

Solution

Creation of audiovisual paratextual material foregrounding integrated inclusiveness of theatrical productions by using blended mix of semiotic systems within paratext itself

Example

Red Earth theatre company (UK Midlands): Accessible paratext explaining/promoting creative captioning

Implication

Ubiquity of paratextual materials in 21st century digital/cultural landscape NOT a given - from perspective of visually/hearing impaired audiences, number/nature of thresholds may be relatively limited
