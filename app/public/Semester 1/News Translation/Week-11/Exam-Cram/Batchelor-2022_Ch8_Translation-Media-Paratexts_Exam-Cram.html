1. Core Definitions

G√©rard Genette (1980s) - Original Framework

Definition: Paratext = what "enables a text to become a book"

Key Innovation: Literary work = TEXT + PARATEXT (not text alone)

Element Type Examples
Peritext Attached to text Title, preface, illustrations, binding, typesetting
Epitext External to text Author interviews, reviews, author's gender

Constraint: Connection to authorial intention/responsibility

Jonathan Gray (2010) - Audiovisual Adaptation

Book: "Show Sold Separately"

Core Quote: "Decisions on what to watch, what not to watch, and how to watch are often made while consuming hype, synergy, and promos, so that by the time we actually encounter 'the show itself,' we have already begun to decode it"

Examples: Ads, trailers, interviews, reviews, merchandising, fan creations, posters, games, spinoffs

2. Key Differences: Genette vs Gray

Three Major Alterations

Aspect Genette (Literary) Gray (Audiovisual)
Producer Author (authorial intention) Industry-created + Viewer-created
Hierarchy Text = core, paratext = threshold Fluid (can reverse - e.g., game mods become central)
Timing Before encountering text "In medias res" - during extended engagement

Alternative Metaphors

  • Genette: "Threshold" (linear progression)
  • Boni (2016): "Media ecosystem"
  • Jones (2008): "Universe"
  • Consalvo (2017): "Flexibility in when a game text might become a paratext and vice versa"

5. Theoretical Debates

‚ö†Ô∏è The Boundary Problem

Question: "Where is the line between paratext and context?"

Risk: Paratext risks "collapsing into the vastness of 'the context'" (Rockenberger 2014)

Example: Is person recommending film in conversation a "paratext"?

Proposed Solutions

  1. Batchelor (2018): "A consciously crafted threshold for a text which has the potential to influence the way(s) in which the text is received"
    • Maintains distinction between context/happenstance and paratext
    • BUT: Thorny difficulties in application
  2. Wittgensteinian Approach: Define by examples ("this and similar things are paratexts") rather than fixed boundaries
    • Case-by-case basis
    • Genette himself: "however indeterminable its boundaries, the paratext retains at its centre a distinctive and undisputed territory"

Why Use the Term "Paratext"?

Benefit 1 - Validation: Grouping ancillary materials under umbrella term validates research into apparently secondary/ephemeral materials (Gray 2015: "We need the word as a reminder ‚Äì an insistence, even ‚Äì to look at paratexts")

Benefit 2 - Interdisciplinary Dialogue: Facilitates deeper dialogue with media/digital studies where theoretical underpinnings rigorously developed

6. Methodological Challenges

Challenge 1: Variability

Problem: Digital paratexts offer multiple entry points - impossible to assume any given viewer encountered any particular paratext

Examples:

  • Viewers may access through official website, fan video, or online review
  • News accessible through Twitter, embedded links, or homepage

Personalization Issue (Gray on Netflix): "We're all getting potentially really different experiences of texts... the flow that Netflix gives me is not the flow that Netflix gives you"

Solutions to Variability

  • Nottingham-Martin (2014): Hypothetical "what if" model (what if reader experiences paratext before/during/after text?)
  • Gray: Distinguish "loud" (unavoidable) vs "quiet" (less accessible) paratexts
  • Stewart (2010): Incorporate study of flows to ascertain which paratexts viewers actually access

Challenge 2: Ephemerality

Two Types:

  1. Short Duration: Channel idents (15-25 seconds) - BUT watched thousands of times
  2. Short Availability: Bus posters (week or two per film)

Gray (2016) on Mad Men:

  • Libraries hold product itself, NOT paratexts
  • Future analysts won't have access to all relevant material
  • Conclusion: Reliability of analyses of past media products must be cast into doubt

Solutions to Ephemerality

  • Archiving strategies: Internet studies methods (Br√ºgger 2011)
  • Physical locatedness: Researcher based in target culture OR dedicated fieldwork time
  • Challenge: Researcher may not even be aware ephemeral paratexts exist

7. Future Directions

Potential Benefits for AVT Research

  1. Increased focus on promotional/ancillary materials ‚Üí fuller audience perception accounts
  2. Holistic approach: fan-created + viewer-created + industry-created under one umbrella
  3. Greater attention to agency and power dynamics
  4. Greater attention to economic factors (investment ‚Üî quantity/quality of paratexts ‚Üî success)

Potential Benefits for News Translation

  • Multiple thresholds to online news all influence decisions AND interpretations
  • Many thresholds haven't drawn sustained attention - power relatively uninterrogated
  • Umbrella term for holistic approach (not just "headlines")
  • Agency/economics brought further to fore

Note: Less clear than AVT (limited take-up in communication studies too)
