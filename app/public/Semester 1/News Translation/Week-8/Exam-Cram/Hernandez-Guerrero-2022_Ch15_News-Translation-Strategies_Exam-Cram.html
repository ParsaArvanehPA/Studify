‚ö° HIGH-PROBABILITY EXAM TOPICS

1. DOMINANT MACROSTRATEGY: DOMESTICATION

  • WHY: Audience must understand message efficiently with maximum clarity
  • EVIDENCE: Bassnett (2005), Bielsa & Bassnett (2009), Hern√°ndez Guerrero (2009), Holland (2013)
  • QUOTE: "The dominant strategy is ABSOLUTE DOMESTICATION" (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009:10)
  • REASON: Foreignization is detrimental to understanding (Bassnett 2005)

2. THE FOUR CORE MICROSTRATEGIES

  1. OMISSION/DELETION - Removing information (KEY STRATEGY per Bielsa & Bassnett 2009)
  2. ADDITION - Adding background information
  3. SUBSTITUTION - Replacing elements
  4. REORGANIZATION/PERMUTATION - Changing structure/order

Supporters: Vuorinen (1995), Hursti (2001), Van Dijk (1988)

‚ö° EXAM TIP: Know the difference between Fujii's view (gatekeeping goes BEYOND translation) vs. Vuorinen's view (gatekeeping operations are PART AND PARCEL of normal translation)

Gatekeeping: Two-Level Model (Valde√≥n 2016)

Level Who Function When
Institutional Media organization Select material that can/should be translated BEFORE translation
Individual Journalist-translator Select info to disseminate; make decisions on omissions, additions, adaptations DURING translation

Transediting: Three Types (Stetting 1989)

Type Description
Cleaning-up Adaptation to standard of efficiency in expression
Situational Adaptation to intended function in new social contexts
Cultural Adaptation to needs and conventions of target culture

Risk Management: Four Strategies (Pym & Matsushita 2015-2018)

Strategy Description
Risk Taking Accepting potential negative consequences
Risk Avoidance Completely avoiding risky elements
Risk Transfer Shifting responsibility for risk
Risk Mitigation Accepting one risk while protecting against consequences by incurring second risk
‚ö° EXAM TIP: Risk mitigation "challenges the huge binarisms [foreignizing/domesticating]" (Pym & Matsushita 2018:15)

‚ùì High-Probability Exam Questions

Question 1: Explain the dominant macrostrategy in news translation. Why is it used? Provide evidence from scholars.

Answer Framework:

  • DOMESTICATION is dominant (Bassnett 2005; Bielsa & Bassnett 2009)
  • WHY: Audience must understand efficiently; foreignization detrimental to understanding
  • EVIDENCE: "Absolute domestication" quote (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009:10)
  • Example: Saddam Hussein speeches - highlighting foreignness reinforces negative perceptions

Question 2: What are the four core microstrategies in news translation? Provide scholars who support this framework.

Answer Framework:

  • 1. OMISSION/DELETION - key strategy (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009)
  • 2. ADDITION - background information
  • 3. SUBSTITUTION - replacing elements
  • 4. REORGANIZATION - changing structure
  • SCHOLARS: Vuorinen (1995), Hursti (2001), Van Dijk (1988)

Question 3: Contrast Fujii's and Vuorinen's views on gatekeeping in news translation.

Answer Framework:

  • FUJII (1988): Gatekeeping goes BEYOND mere translation; large-scale manipulation
  • VUORINEN (1995): Gatekeeping operations are PART AND PARCEL of normal translation
  • IMPLICATION: Different views on whether these operations are exceptional or normal

Question 4: How are quotations translated in news? Explain the different approaches in agencies vs. newspapers.

Answer Framework:

  • AGENCIES: Quote whenever possible; idiomatic translation (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009)
  • SPECIAL CASE: More LITERAL for quotes/signed dispatches (Davier 2012) - fear of mistranslation
  • When literal impossible: OMIT or PARAPHRASE
  • MOST FREQUENT: Omission (Matsushita 2014) - risk management
  • IDEOLOGICAL: Selection/addition/deletion reflects ideology (Chen 2009)

Question 5: Discuss the impact of digitalization and media convergence on news translation strategies.

Answer Framework:

  • TREND: Possible decrease in translation use
  • Davier (2019): Audiovisual production AVOIDS translation
  • Hern√°ndez Guerrero & D√≠az L√≥pez (2020): SUBSTITUTION strategy - omit or replace with target content
  • NEW PRACTICES: Tweets as quotes; screenshots make translation VISIBLE (Hern√°ndez Guerrero 2020)
  • CHALLENGES: Texts updated repeatedly; fragmented genres (Caimotto 2019)

Question 6: Explain the risk management framework in news translation. What are the four strategies?

Answer Framework:

  • NEW PERSPECTIVE: Strategies = ways of MANAGING RISK, not achieving equivalence
  • Four strategies: Risk taking, avoidance, transfer, MITIGATION
  • MITIGATION: Accept one risk while protecting against consequences (Pym & Matsushita 2018)
  • Example: Omit difficult words in quotes to avoid risk of confusion
  • IMPLICATION: Challenges domestication/foreignization binary

Question 7: Why are headlines often substituted rather than translated? Provide evidence.

Answer Framework:

  • Each language has OWN headlining conventions
  • Audiences accustomed to their textual conventions
  • "Only rarely transferred intact" (Sidiropoulou 1995)
  • Headlines = "re-capturing, reframing, re-narrating" (Kaniklidou & House 2013)
  • Example: El Pa√≠s Bolsonaro controversy - different headlines for Spanish vs. Brazilian editions

Question 8: What methodological challenges face news translation research? What solution is proposed?

Answer Framework:

  • PROBLEM: Textual analysis alone INSUFFICIENT
  • Don't know institutional constraints or individual choices
  • SOLUTION: TRIANGULATION (Davier, Sch√§ffner & van Doorslaer 2018)
  • Combine textual analysis + fieldwork (interviews, observation, internal documents)
  • Hypotheses confirmed/amended through first-hand information
