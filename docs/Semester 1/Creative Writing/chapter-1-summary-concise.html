CHAPTER 1: PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
Quick Exam Summary

PARAGRAPH: A group of related sentences that discusses ONE main idea. Length varies, but must be long enough to develop the idea clearly.

2. TOPIC SENTENCES

TOPIC SENTENCE = TOPIC + CONTROLLING IDEA
States the main idea of the paragraph (usually first sentence)

Two Essential Parts:

  • TOPIC: What the paragraph is about (subject)
  • CONTROLLING IDEA: Limits/controls the topic to a specific area
Example Topic Controlling Idea
Convenience foods are easy to prepare. Convenience foods are easy to prepare
Driving on freeways requires skill and alertness. Driving on freeways requires skill and alertness

Common Problems to Avoid:

‚úó TOO GENERAL
"English has been influenced by other languages."
Fix: Add specific controlling idea
‚úó TOO SPECIFIC
"The slang so long comes from Arabic salaam."
Fix: Broaden to cover multiple examples
‚úó INCOMPLETE
"The Arabic origin of English words."
Fix: Make it a complete sentence
‚úó UNRELATED IDEAS
Multiple controlling ideas in one sentence
Fix: Focus on ONE idea only

3. SUPPORTING SENTENCES

PURPOSE: Explain or prove the topic sentence with SPECIFIC DETAILS

4. CONCLUDING SENTENCES

TWO PURPOSES:
‚ë† Signal the end of paragraph
‚ë° Remind reader of important ideas

Two Methods:

Method 1: SUMMARIZE
Review main supporting points
Example: "These examples show that..."
Method 2: PARAPHRASE
Restate topic sentence differently
Repeat main idea in new words

Signal Words:

With comma: In brief, In conclusion, Indeed, In short, Finally, Therefore, Thus, To sum up
Without comma: The evidence suggests that..., These examples show that..., We can see that...

‚úó NEVER introduce a NEW idea in the concluding sentence!

6. THE WRITING PROCESS (6 Steps)

Step Action
1. Prewrite Brainstorm ideas (listing technique) - don't worry about grammar
2. Organize Group related ideas, choose ONE main idea, select supporting points
3. First Draft Write topic sentence + support + conclusion - focus on IDEAS not perfection
4. Revise Check CONTENT & ORGANIZATION (get peer review)
5. Edit Check GRAMMAR, MECHANICS, SPELLING (use checklist)
6. Final Draft Include all changes, proofread again, submit

8. QUICK EXAM TIPS

Memory Aid: SPECS for Topic Sentences

  • Specific enough (not too broad)
  • Provides direction (has controlling idea)
  • Expressible in one sentence
  • Clear and complete
  • Single focus (one main idea)

The 3-2-1 Support Rule

  • 3 types of support (example + fact + quotation)
  • 2 sentences minimum per point
  • 1 clear connection to topic sentence

Common Errors to Avoid

  • ‚úó The "List" paragraph (no development, just lists items)
  • ‚úó New ideas in conclusion (stick to what you've discussed!)
  • ‚úó Drifting off-topic (every sentence must relate to controlling idea)
