Translating Foreign Articles with Local Implications: A Case Study

Author: M. Cristina Caimotto
Source: Schäffner & Bassnett (2010), Chapter 3
Focus: News Translation, Media Manipulation, Italian Press, Translation-Reporting

1. Introduction & Research Focus

Central Research Question

This chapter analyzes how Italian newspapers reported, commented on, and translated a Financial Times article by Wolfgang Munchau about Italy's 2006 election results. The study demonstrates how a foreign article was manipulated and turned into a completely different message to fit local political narratives.

Author's Disclaimer

The goal is NOT to argue for Prodi or Berlusconi, nor to criticize journalism. The point is to show HOW and WHY a concept in a source text was turned into a different concept in the target text from a Translation Studies perspective.

1.1 Background: Italian Practice

It is common practice for Italian journalists to report and comment on articles published abroad about Italy. Readers are interested and curious about what foreign journalists think of them.

  • Since The Economist (2001) published strongly critical articles about Berlusconi, foreign journalists' words are often reported in Italian newspapers the next day
  • Articles are sometimes translated, sometimes integrated with comments, sometimes briefly quoted
  • The translation process is hardly ever made visible

3. Munchau's Original Article

3.1 Publication Details

  • Date: April 17, 2006 (one week after elections)
  • Title: "Prodi's lamentable poll is bad news for the euro"
  • Author: Wolfgang Munchau (German origin)
  • Position: Associate editor, Financial Times
  • Column: Weekly column about EU and European economy
  • Publication: Financial Times

3.2 Opening Sentence (CRITICAL!)

ST: "The narrow election victory by Romano Prodi's centre-left alliance was the worst imaginable outcome in terms of Italy's chances to remain in the eurozone beyond 2015."


KEY POINT: The problem is the NARROW victory, NOT that Prodi won!

3.3 Article Characteristics

  • Technical language: Financial newspaper, quite technical terms
  • Complex issues: Requires knowledge of how financial markets work
  • Not smooth reading: Difficult for average reader
  • Focus: Economic analysis, NOT political statement

Example of Technical Complexity

"From an investor's viewpoint, Italian withdrawal from the eurozone is equivalent to sovereign default. Given this outlook, why are financial markets not yet speculating on such an event? Last week, yields on Italian 10-year government bonds traded at only 0.3 percentage points above the yields of equivalent German bonds..."

4. How Italian Newspapers Reported It

4.1 Introduction of New Term: "Translation-Reporting"

Definition

Translation-Reporting: A term introduced by Caimotto to describe the various ways of (re)presenting foreign articles, ranging from:

  • Proper translation (Jakobson 1959)
  • Transediting (Hursti 2001)
  • Reporting comments that refer to ST without quoting it

4.2 Corriere della Sera (Centre-Right)

Political Orientation

  • Of the three main Italian dailies, leans most toward right
  • During election campaign, openly declared preference for Prodi over Berlusconi

Online Version Analysis

Characteristics:

  • Nearest to "translation proper" compared to other Italian articles
  • Presented as Italian journalist's comment on Munchau's editorial
  • Reports in Italian everything from ST, omitting only ONE sentence
  • Passages from FT in inverted commas BUT never states explicitly they're translated
  • Unclear that basically whole article is reported in Italian

Heading Strategy

Italian heading: "Voto: l'Italia rischia l'uscita dall'euro"
(Vote: Italy risks getting out of the euro)


Problem: Heading in inverted commas but NOT actually quoted from ST - journalist reporting what they understood was Munchau's opinion

Embedded Comments & Functions

Functions of Italian Journalist's Interventions

  1. Signal reporting: Indicate they're reporting someone else's words
  2. Distance from opinion: Can be interpreted as distancing from Munchau's opinion
  3. Guide readers: Help understand topic through paragraph headings
  4. Frame authority: MOST INTERESTING - Positively frame FT/Munchau authority, making constructed negative opinion of Prodi's victory more authoritative
  5. Add reactions: Insert information about reactions (e.g., EU Commission response)

Translation Quality Issues

Problems with Propositional Meaning (Baker 1992):

  • "Sensible" → "sensibile" (should be different Italian word; "sensibile" = sensitive)
  • "Default" in heading: In non-technical Italian, only used for computerized processes; should be "insolvenza"
  • Technical terminology: Difficult reading, average reader likely stops after first paragraphs

Likely Reader Inference

Average reader reads first few lines, stops when financial details become too technical, infers that Financial Times is against Prodi, just as they knew The Economist is against Berlusconi.

Printed Version (Next Day)

  • Section: "Economy - currency" (not politics)
  • More readable: Target readership more familiar with financial issues
  • Focus: Italy remaining in eurozone
  • BUT: Still part of political interpretation

Reference to The Economist

"L'analisi del quotidiano inglese, sempre molto severo sulle questioni italiane, è caduta come il sale sulle ferite ancora aperte dal periodo post-elettorale..."

Translation: "The English daily's analysis, always very severe about Italian issues, has fallen like salt on the still open wounds left after the post-electoral period..."

Implication: Clear reference to articles against Berlusconi by The Economist

CRITICAL LINGUISTIC ANALYSIS: Adjective Position

The Problem with "la risicata vittoria"

ST: "The narrow election victory"

Italian TT: "La risicata vittoria"

Italian Adjective Rules (Serianni 2000)

  • Adjective BEFORE noun: Descriptive function, stylistic sophistication, emotional emphasis
  • Adjective AFTER noun: Restrictive function, identifies/specifies

Serianni's Example

"Le vecchie tubature hanno ceduto"
(The old pipes have burst - descriptive, all old pipes)

"Le tubature vecchie hanno ceduto"
(Only the old ones burst, not the new ones - restrictive)

Consequence in Translation

  • "La risicata vittoria": Simply describes victory quality (enriching description)
  • "La vittoria risicata": Would identify victory as NARROW ONE, making clear problem is narrowness, NOT that Prodi won

Effect Obtained: Implies "pitch black forecast" is consequence of Prodi winning rather than Berlusconi, NOT what Munchau meant (that narrow victory is negative, wider would be better).

Was This Deliberate?

  • Likely NOT deliberate but due to time pressure and source language influence
  • Adjective position is problematic even for native speakers (Accademia della Crusca FAQ exists on this topic)
  • Still example of sloppiness contributing to distortion
  • Professional translator expected to work properly under pressure and avoid such distortions

4.3 La Repubblica (Centre-Left)

Political Orientation

Moderately left-leaning national newspaper

Published Short News Item (April 18, 2006)

Content: Reporting Prodi's declaration that FT was NOT criticizing Prodi's government but Berlusconi's exclusively

"Il Financial Times non sta sparando sul governo Prodi, ma esclusivamente sul governo Berlusconi... Se la situazione è difficile è per colpa del governo Berlusconi."

Translation: "Financial Times is not shooting at Prodi's government, but exclusively at Berlusconi's government... If the situation is difficult it is the fault of Berlusconi's government."

4.4 La Stampa (Centre)

Political Orientation

Third national newspaper, political attitude between Corriere and Repubblica

Article Characteristics:

  • Much smaller number of quotations from FT text
  • Journalist intervenes more to explain, summarize, add information
  • Most added information reports various politician comments

Prodi's Reaction (via spokesperson Sircana)

"Sappiamo che i margini in cui opereremo sono stretti, ma il Paese sarebbe diviso in due anche se il risultato del voto fosse stato 52% contro 48%... la comunità internazionale ha generalmente visto con favore il fatto che in Italia abbia vinto Prodi e non Berlusconi"

Translation: "We know margins are narrow, but country would be split even if result had been 52% vs 48%... international community has generally welcomed that Prodi won rather than Berlusconi"

Analysis: Reply confounds two issues (wide vs. narrow AND left vs. right). Last statement re-enters frame of "Prodi vs. Berlusconi" rather than addressing narrow victory issue.

4.5 Follow-up Coverage

Corriere Website (Next Day)

  • Published list of politician comments defending or condemning Prodi
  • NO summary of Munchau's article - assumes reader already knows
  • Defenders disagree with Munchau's thesis
  • Attackers state FT position is correct
  • Shows: Original article inaccessible to most readers for linguistic reasons, twisted message more interesting to Italian readers looking for foreign comments on election results

5. Causes of Manipulation

5.1 Time Pressure and Sloppiness

  • Key constraint in news translation process
  • Cannot be held as SINGLE cause for faulty translations
  • Professional translators expected to work properly under pressure

5.2 Infotainment in Italian Media

Definition

Infotainment: Mixing of information and entertainment

"Journalism has always entertained as well as informed. Had it not done so it would not have reached a mass audience. But today, say journalism critics, the instinct to amuse is driving out the will, and depleting the resource, to report and analyse in any depth. Obsessed with a world of celebrity and trivia, the news media are rotting our brains and undermining our civic life." (Hargreaves 2003)

Italy's Situation

  • Tendency toward infotainment stronger than other countries
  • Widely held: Consequence of Berlusconi's wide control over Italian media
  • 1984: Berlusconi broadcast commercial TV nationally (breaking laws), competing with RAI
  • Market techniques implemented in media business and political campaign

5.3 Loporcaro's Analysis: News as Narration vs. Information

Two Conceptions of News (Loporcaro 2005)

1. Traditional: News as Information

  • Information about any new event
  • Pivotal role in democratic society
  • Fundamental condition for citizens to participate and control democracy

2. Modern: News as Narration

  • News constitutes whole narration with function of offering stories to society
  • Continues ancestral narration expressed in popular cultures through myth (Lule 2001)
  • In this case study: FT article becomes new episode in which centre-left is attacked by British, after 5 years when "foreigners" (The Economist) were always against Berlusconi

5.4 Historical Context: "Questione della Lingua"

The Language Issue (started 1500)

  • Debate favoring employment of old classical variety of Italian
  • Went on through centuries
  • Left large part of population out of public discussion
  • Italy already had lower percentage of readers than other European countries
  • 1970s-1980s: Massification in school programs and public language
  • Result: General weakening of "margins" guaranteeing quality in journalistic practice

Breeding Ground: This situation creates breeding ground for journalism that prefers sensationalism to precision and allows foreign articles to be reported and distorted without any reaction from other media.

5.5 Translation as Extra Filter

When object of discussion is inspired by foreign text, necessity for translation introduces extra filter:

  • With domestic issues, different news providers point out distortions → at least 2 versions available
  • With foreign text requiring translation → NO alternative translation proposed
  • Impossible to establish from text-based analysis whether distortion introduced by translator or journalist
  • Main issue: TIME pressure
  • First twisting likely consequence of time pressure + lack of professional training

Key Problem

Lack of recognition of translator's role. Available studies suggest translation of news often carried out by "language experts" trained mainly as journalists, NOT professional translators.

6. Theoretical Implications

6.1 Terminology Challenges

Open Question for Translation Studies

Should we create new labels for translation practices that don't fit "translation proper"?

  • Localization
  • Transediting
  • Language mediation
  • Translation-reporting (introduced in this chapter)

OR is label "translation" wide enough to include phenomena like this case study?

6.2 News Translation Studies

  • Analysis of news as object of inquiry for TS only started in recent years
  • Translation practices in newsrooms raise many important issues
  • Need to tackle these to better understand implications for discipline
  • Lot of research still needed in this field

7. Key Findings & Conclusions

7.1 Main Findings

  1. Range of translation practices: From proper translation to transediting to reporting
  2. Manipulation for narrative: Foreign article twisted to fit ongoing Italian political narrative
  3. No counter-narrative: Media kept concentrating on wrong point without questioning Italian interpretation
  4. Readers don't check ST: Journalists take advantage that few Italian readers will check source text
  5. Infotainment culture: Shallow attitude widespread in Italian information system

7.2 Value of Research

  • Not only analysis of news translation practices
  • Broader analysis of Italian mainstream media
  • Example of journalists taking advantage of linguistic barriers
  • News Translation researcher = insider AND outsider simultaneously
  • Great potential for interdisciplinarity
  • Complete picture of various news translation practices

7.3 Professional Implications

Consequences & The Way Forward

  • Phenomenon can occur with domestic issues, but different providers point out distortion
  • With foreign texts, translation introduces extra filter without alternative versions
  • Need for professional translator recognition in news industry
  • Need for training in translating under tight deadlines
  • More research needed on journalistic translation practices
