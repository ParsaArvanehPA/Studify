‚ö° Speed & Accuracy

EXAM KEY CONCEPT: Speed = PART OF QUALITY (not opposed to it!)

"To translate means to translate fast, well and accurately" - AFP editor

"Speed is as important as quality, it is part of quality" - IPS senior member
Historical context:
  • Reuters: 70-min scoop on Gandhi shooting (1948)
  • AFP: 56-min lead on Israeli hostages death (Munich 1972)
  • Old tactics: bribing telegraph officials, blocking phones, pretending phones broken
Contemporary pressure:
  • 24/7 continuous channels
  • Near-instantaneous coverage possible
  • Must satisfy varied client needs any time
  • Increasing acceleration of information circulation

THE PARADOX: Translator's transformative power = ability to change angle/POV to make text function effectively for different public

Pushes equivalence to its limit - shows how different versions of global news function in diverse local contexts

Example: Reagan's Funeral

Problem: English notes from Washington celebrated Reagan. But in Latin America = associated with dirty wars in Central America (Nicaragua/El Salvador/Guatemala)

Solution: Add background on economic policies + war participation (more significant than "ex-president's last look of love to his wife")

üåê English Hegemony

AFP:
  • Anglophone editor-in-chief appointed (1999)
  • Harmonization favors Anglophone model: shorter, more concise, direct (vs French/Spanish rhetoric/literary style)
  • Related to expansion in North American/Asian markets (1980s+)
IPS:
  • Theory: English + Spanish equal. Practice: "global service" = English; Spanish = "regional"
  • English replacing Spanish as internal working language
  • Global meetings now in English (used to require Spanish)
  • Prize for best feature: only English articles/translations can participate

üìö IPS Language Politics - Know This

Objective: "Balanced representation of ethnic diversity and geographical distribution"

Key features:
  • Promotes equality between languages
  • Encourages communication between minority languages
  • First international service in Swahili (1980s)
  • Only agency producing in Nepali, Thai
  • Aim: Local impact, especially Third World
