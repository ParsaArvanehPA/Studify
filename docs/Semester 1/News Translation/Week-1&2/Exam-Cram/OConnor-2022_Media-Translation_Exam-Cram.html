ü§ù Theoretical Frameworks

Entanglement (Hodder, 2011): Humans and things co-constitutive. Dialectic between DEPENDENCE (reliance) and DEPENDENCY (constraint). Objects cannot be studied in isolation.
Actor-Network Theory (Latour): Moves away from subject/object dichotomy. Networks of ACTANTS at human, societal, institutional, material AND technological levels.
Coldiron's Middle Ground: Material and non-material aspects are "fully interpenetrative and mutually animating" - not binary/competitive

üìñ Historical Periods - KNOW THESE

Early Modern Book Production

  • Coldiron (2015): Printing was "co-process" of translation
  • Transformission: Material textuality as co-factor with linguistic factors
  • Paratexts (Genette): Dedications, title pages, footnotes, annotations, illustrations - shaping/framing strategies
  • Multiple agents: printers, translators, patrons

Industrial Print (19th Century)

  • Key feature: SPEED (production, distribution, consumption)
  • Bachleitner (2009): "Translation factories" in German after 1820
  • Littau: Fast reading presupposes readable page + digestible prose
  • Book series: Uniform editions, standardization, homogeneity
  • Impact: Emphasis on readability, comprehensibility, fluency

Periodicals - UNDEREXPLORED

  • Characteristics: Ephemeral, mutable, responsive to forces
  • Translation types: Anonymous, extracts, serialized, experimental, pseudotranslations, adaptations
  • Challenge: Ephemerality = fewer survive + perceived as less valuable
  • Important: Cheap format ‚â† less important - reached wider communities
