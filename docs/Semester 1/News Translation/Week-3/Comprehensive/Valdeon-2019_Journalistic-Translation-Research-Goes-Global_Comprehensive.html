üéØ 1. Introduction & Context

Article Purpose: Reviews publications on journalistic translation research spanning 2015-2020, highlighting main theoretical frameworks and methodological approaches used by researchers.

Background: In 2015, Valde√≥n surveyed 15 years of journalistic translation research. Since then, numerous monographs, edited collections, special issues, and articles have been published, establishing journalistic translation as a consolidated subfield within translation studies.
Journalistic translation research has expanded geographically (from Europe/North America to global), theoretically (multiple frameworks), and methodologically (mixed-method approaches now standard).

Three Main Disciplinary Approaches

  • Discourse Analysis & Linguistics: Language-based, corpus methods, critical discourse analysis
  • Sociology: Narrative theory, Bourdieu's concepts, power relations
  • Communication/Journalism Studies: Framing, gatekeeping, convergence
Important Note: This division is somewhat artificial - many authors combine two or more approaches in their work

Baker's Narrative Theory - FOUNDATIONAL

Source: Baker (2006) adapted Somers & Gibson's narrative theory for translation studies

Key Application: Understanding how narratives are constructed, maintained, and challenged through translation

Scholar(s) Study Key Finding

üì∫ 4. Communication Studies & Journalistic Translation Research

The Dialogue Problem

Border Crossings (Gambier & van Doorslaer, 2016) - House & Loenhoff chapter didn't quite succeed in bridging translation studies and communication studies. Partly due to lack of theorization of "translation" in journalism/communication studies.

Valde√≥n (2018) finding: Journalism scholars use "translation" generically (any transformation) OR word-for-word only. Rarely use contemporary TS concept.

Gatekeeping - CRITICAL CONCEPT

Gatekeeping: Process of selecting/filtering information for publication

Note: Borrowed from communication studies. Never used by journalism scholars themselves (Valde√≥n, 2018)
Scholar Application Finding
Pan (2014) Cankao Xiaoxi (Reference News) Used gatekeeping + "transediting" concepts
Song (2017) Yonhap News Agency (South Korea) Selection/translation of NYT articles - avoids negative representations of Korean government
Valde√≥n (2016a) Theoretical framework Translation = second-order gatekeeping mechanism
Valde√≥n (Forthcoming) First-level gatekeeping Translation as first-level gatekeeping - what gets translated/not translated reflects ideological affinity

Framing - EXTREMELY PRODUCTIVE CONCEPT

Definition: Way of presenting information that shapes audience perception

Xia (2019): "A translator develops a particular conceptualisation of a piece of news or reorients his interpretation of the news item. Decision making in communication affects frames in thought and overall attitudes" (p. 38)

Liu's "Transframing" - NEW CONCEPT

Liu (2017, 2019): Studied Financial Times ‚Üí Cankao Xiaoxi translations

Finding: Cankao Xiaoxi acted as government mouthpiece by selecting only positive China coverage, "strengthening positive frame of Chinese economy through translation of financial news" (2017, p. 460)

Proposed Term: "TRANSFRAMING" to bridge gap between translation + journalism studies, potentially replacing classical "transediting"

Baker vs Communication Studies: Framing Overlap

Framing used in sociology (Baker's narrative theory) AND communication studies (Liu's transframing). Shows interdisciplinary convergence!

Example: Qin & Zhang (2018) - Edward Snowden case: studied selective appropriation + labelling as framing/reframing strategies. English texts ignored China's involvement, Chinese texts condemned US "massive surveillance programme"

Convergence

Convergence (Davier & Conway, 2019): "A chameleon" - news texts adapted to new modes AND different cultural/linguistic environments

Definition (Drawing on Deuze): Cooperation/synergy between different media
Gendron, Conway & Davier (2019): Canadian Broadcasting Corporation (French + English)

Three ways translation appears:
  1. Voice-over in embedded video (speaker audible, journalist translates louder)
  2. Translation of video/sound in accompanying written text
  3. Subtitles in embedded video

Finding: More common in national news (better tech, greater political responsibility). Different visibility in national vs regional news.

Special Issue on Methodology

Across Languages and Cultures (2018): Edited by Davier, Sch√§ffner & van Doorslaer

Goal: Explore "new methodological paths" + "encourage additional research using variety of methods for investigating news translation in its complexity" (p. 161)

The Source Text Problem

Davier & van Doorslaer (2018): "The unique source text has collapsed, and the same goes for the unique author" (p. 242)

Proposed Solution: Compilation of multilingual comparable corpora with traces of translations (drawing on Johansson, 2003)

Characteristic: Content-based corpora

Recommendation: Combine textual with other research methodologies
