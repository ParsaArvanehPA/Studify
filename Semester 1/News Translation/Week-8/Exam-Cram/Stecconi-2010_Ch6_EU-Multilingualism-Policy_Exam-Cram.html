1. Historical Foundation (1957)

‚úì European Integration Origins

  • 1951: European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC)
  • 1957: Treaty of Rome - EEC & Euratom
  • Revolutionary idea: Countries willingly cede sovereign powers to independent authority
  • Goal: Lasting peace through shared industries
CORE PRINCIPLE: Equality - ALL member states have same rights/duties regardless of size (contrast to Congress of Vienna 1814-1914 where larger countries dominated)

2. Legal Basis - Three Key Articles (TEC)

Article 21 - Citizen Communication Rights

What: Citizens can write to EU institutions in ANY official language and receive answer in SAME language

Significance: Makes EU multilingual by law (unique among international organizations)

Article 314 - Equally Authentic Languages

Original (1957): 4 languages (Dutch, French, German, Italian)

Current: 23 languages

Key phrase: "equally authentic" = all language versions have equal legal status

Article 290 - Decision-Making

CRITICAL: Council decides on language matters by UNANIMOUS vote

= Each member state has VETO power on language decisions

3. Council Regulation No. 1 (1958)

First regulation adopted:

  • Article 1: Official and working languages = member state languages
  • Article 3: Documents to member states in their language
  • Problem: NO distinction between "official" and "working" languages

Working Languages Reality

Internal EU communication mainly uses:

  1. English
  2. French
  3. German (distant third)

Result: Passive bilingualism common - staff from 27 nationalities communicate in 2 languages, usually neither is mother tongue

4. The "Equally Authentic" Paradox

‚ö†Ô∏è EXAM CRITICAL CONCEPT

Two interpretations:

  1. Focus on "equally": No language has higher status than others
  2. Focus on "authentic": Translations NOT regarded as translations (to avoid legal challenges)

Implicit view: Translation = corrupting operation producing second-rate texts

Why problematic: Could freeze trials, prevent implementation, fuel nationalist objections

5. Policy Evolution

Key Milestones

Year Development
1957 Original treaties - NO language provisions
1958 Regulation No. 1 - language as administrative issue
1992-93 Maastricht Treaty - language via education (Art. 149)
2004 Constitutional Treaty - language as citizenship right
2005 "New Framework Strategy for Multilingualism"
2007 Commissioner Leonard Orban - dedicated language portfolio

6. 2005 Multilingualism Strategy

Three Definitions of "Multilingualism"

  1. Person's ability to use several languages
  2. Co-existence of language communities in one area
  3. EU policy field promoting expression of all languages

Criticism: Overstretched term - should use distinct terms

Three Policy Aims

  1. Promoting language learning & linguistic diversity
  2. Promoting multilingual economy
  3. Making EU legislation available in all official languages

7. Three Major Changes in European Linguistic Landscape

1. Migration: Adds new customs/languages to European diversity
2. Erasmus & Internal Mobility: Easy cross-border study/work - mixes existing palette to create new colors
3. Devolution to Regions: Multiple allegiances (region, country, EU) - regional languages gain status

8. Language Services

DGT - Directorate General for Translation (Largest)

2007 Statistics:

  • 2,300 staff (1,700 full-time translators)
  • 2006: 1.5 million pages translated
  • 20% by freelancers

Why essential: Prerequisite for proper functioning + democratic legitimacy

9. Language Learning Policy

‚ö° "MOTHER TONGUE PLUS TWO"

Commission recommendation: Each EU citizen should speak TWO foreign languages + mother tongue

EU vs USA Approach

USA EU
English-only Multilingual diversity
Melting pot - assimilation Welding - pieces remain distinct

English Dominance Reality (Eurobarometer 2006)

  • 38% of non-English Europeans can hold conversation in English
  • NOT a behemoth - many learn neighbor languages
  • German popular in Central/Eastern Europe
  • Italian in Malta/Croatia
  • Russian in former Baltic Republics/Bulgaria

‚ö†Ô∏è Policy Warnings

  1. Design language policy as unobtrusive as possible
  2. AVOID top-down approaches (associated with totalitarian regimes)
  3. Languages = identity - people resist abrupt policies
  4. Go along with observed behavior, involve people transparently

10. Linguistic Diversity Complexity

Scale of Diversity

  • Aboriginal languages in EU: ~90
  • Example - UK survey (2004-05): 273 languages spoken by school children
  • Migrant languages: DWARF aboriginal languages in number

Policy Imperative

ANY language policy should protect and develop ALL languages within EU borders:

  • National languages
  • Regional/minority languages
  • Migrant languages

Priority: Communities that MOST need support
