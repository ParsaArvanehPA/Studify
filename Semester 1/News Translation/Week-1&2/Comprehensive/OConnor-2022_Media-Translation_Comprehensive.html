üì± Media and Translation: Historical Intersections

Exam-Oriented Summary | Anne O'Connor (2022)

üé¨ 1. Core Argument

Central Thesis: Media technology impacts translation processes, forms, and meanings. The medium of communication is NOT just a container - it shapes translation practices and products.
This chapter examines how MEDIA OF COMPOSITION and MEDIA OF TRANSMISSION impact the eventual form and meaning of translation products

Key Terms

Materiality: Physical form of communication (vellum, paper, digital)

Mediality: The form of communication used to create, contain and transmit a translation

Inclusive understanding of media: Encompasses vellum, paper, analogue, digital, AND human forms of communication

üìö 2. Theoretical Background (1950s-1960s)

Marshall McLuhan (1962, 1967)

Famous Assertion: "The medium is the message"

Argument:
  • Media technology by which content is stored/transmitted has MOST significant consequences for society/culture
  • Importance of media goes beyond content it carries
  • Technicity and mediality of form shape BOTH transmission AND reception

D. F. McKenzie (1999) - FOUNDATIONAL

"Forms effect meaning"
Had fundamental impact on media studies. Argued for importance of 'non-authorial textual determinants' and necessity to consider text as complex structure embracing formal and physical presentation (McKenzie 2002)

Jerome McGann (1988)

Contribution: Drew attention to complex network of people, materials, and events in literary production

Argument: Literature is collaborative art and social process, with input from publishers, editors, designers, printers

Key Convergence

Roger Chartier (1989): "Any comprehension of a writing, no matter what kind it is, depends on the forms in which it reaches the reader" (p. 161)
Material carriers impact on communicative process in BOTH creation AND reception. Historians of book + media theorists argue form is AGENTIVE in creating meaning

üî¨ 3. Media and Translation Studies

Karin Littau - LEADING SCHOLAR

Rallying call (2011, 2016): Attention to material forms of communication and translation

Key Questions Posed (2011: 262):
  1. What role has media technology played in history of translation?
  2. What impact have media transitions had on translation activity per se?
  3. What kinds of translation practices can be associated with different media cultures and their particular hardwares?
Media "actively shape our perceptions and consequently also our mindsets, not through the content they carry, but through their material and technical properties"
(Littau, 2016: 87, italics in original)

Littau's Core Argument

Assertion: Translation bears traces of its particular technological environment (2011: 277)

Example: Scribal culture of textual transcription (letter by letter) ‚Üí linked to literalism in word-for-word translation practices

Via Nietzsche: Writing tools work on our thoughts
"If we take seriously the entanglement of the material and the ideational, it is just as untenable to prioritize spirit over matter or subject over object as it is to downgrade media technologies to empty shells... Media are not merely instruments with which writers or translators produce meanings; rather, they set the framework within which something like meaning becomes possible at all."
(Littau, 2016: 83, italics in original)

Other Key Scholars

Scholar Contribution
Michael Cronin (2003) Impossible to conceive translation outside its object-world. Attention to "translation and things"
Anne Coldiron (2015, 2016) Translations acquire significance in connection to material page and medium
D'hulst (2012) Clustering of transfer must address agents, carriers, source/target poles, products, techniques

‚öñÔ∏è 4. The Great Debate: Agency of Media

Central question: To what degree does media SHAPE/DETERMINE translation? How much agency does media have?

Verbs Used = Degree of Agency

Media described as:
  • Inflecting (least agency)
  • Influencing
  • Catalysing (Coldiron: media intersect with and catalyse effects)
  • Shaping (Littau's preference)
  • Determining (most agency)
  • Constructing

Critiques of Littau

Scholar Response/Critique
Armstrong (2016) Technologies REFLECT textual practices rather than shape them. Practices remain consistent, physical support evolves (p. 104)
Cronin (2017) Extraneous political factors impinge on meaning
Coldiron (2016) Cultural matrices need emphasis
Bachleitner (2016) Don't forget human agents/institutions. Media "do not develop by themselves in mystical way" (p. 108)
Echoes debates against McLuhan's "technological determinism" - similar to Raymond Williams' critique that McLuhan couldn't encompass power, politics, institutions, society

ü§ù 5. Theoretical Frameworks: Human-Object Nexus

New Materialism

Composite sub-fields: Thing Theory, Actor-Network Theory, New Formalism, Object-Oriented Ontology, Posthumanism

Core principle: Objects NOT inert. They exert (determining) influence on form and meaning

Entanglement (Ian Hodder, 2011, 2012)

Definition: Condition of being in the world, linking humans and things where both are co-constitutive

Key insight: "Human-thing entanglement comes about as result of dialectic between:
  • Dependence: Reliance of humans and things on each other
  • Dependency: Constraining and limiting need of humans for things (2011: 175)

Important: Object cannot be studied in isolation - assemblages and networks mean entities are entangled, co-dependent

Actor-Network Theory (ANT) - Latour (1996, 2007)

Moves away from dichotomies:
  • Subject/object
  • Human/non-human

Instead: Networks of determinants or ACTANTS, encompassing agency at human, societal, institutional, material AND technological levels

Appeal for translation: Non-linearity seems receptive to multi-layered processes of meaning and hybridity inherent in translation
Burkette (2016): "Not only should message and media be considered discursive partners, the translator also is part of the same ongoing cycle of co-creation" (p. 320)

Coldiron's Position - Middle Ground

"To me the material and non-material aspects of both translation and media seem to stand not in a binary and competitive relation, and not only in a partly dialectical one [...] but, rather, as fully interpenetrative and mutually animating"
(Coldiron, 2016: 98)

Sensory Engagement

Emerging element: Haptic level interaction with media - swiping, turning page, touching, scrolling

Phenomenological engagement: Sensorial interaction helps create understanding of object/subject dichotomy (Bodei, 2015)

Focus: Lives of objects - audiences, users, social careers, places they go, ways put to use (Meyer et al., 2010)

üìñ 6. Early Modern Book Production

Early modern scholars have led the way in theorizing material dimensions of texts - possibly because smaller number of texts and gaps in historical information

Anne Coldiron (2015): "Printers without Borders"

Innovation: Explored intersections between translation and textuality in Renaissance

Beyond source-target binaries: Considered both verbal AND material elements

Translation processes identified: Catenary, radiant, compressed - patterns that "assume complex transformission as fundamental textual condition" (2019: 208)

Key finding: Printing was a "co-process" of translation

Multi-layered Contributions

Identified contributions from:
  • Printers
  • Translators
  • Patrons

Through: prefaces, typefaces, mise-en-page, title pages, page layout, illustrations, ornamentation

Result: These are elements of translational process - traces found in material form

Transformission (Randall McLeod)

Transformission: "Asks us to consider material textuality as co-factor in translation, concomitant with verbal or linguistic factors" (Coldiron, 2019: 201)

Enables: Reading translation in more than one direction

Key project: "Translation and the Making of Early Modern English Print Culture (1473-1660)" - Belle & Hosington (2017, 2018)

Paratexts (Genette, 1997)

Elements studied:
  • Dedications, title pages
  • Footnotes, annotations, illustrations
  • Prefaces, prologues, epilogues
  • Blank spaces

Significance: Shaping and framing strategies reveal:
  • Variability of liminal printed spaces
  • "Plasticity of paratextual space" (Belle & Hosington, 2018: 4)
  • Instability of material space

Important: Early modern practices complicate Genette's unidirectional understanding (Belle & Hosington, 2018: 9)

Contribution of Early Modernists

  • Examine translations in their TOTALITY
  • Consider changes during "transformission"
  • Emphasize PROCESS of translation
  • Highlight work of multiple agents (editors, printers, booksellers)
  • Move from source/target binaries
  • View texts as multi-layered, evolving creations
  • Demonstrate importance of material dimensions + linguistic aspects
  • Ability to "read" text in multiple directions

    • üè≠ 7. Translation and Industrial Print (19th Century)

    Key Material Changes

    Technological innovations:
    • Steam press
    • Pulp paper
    • Transport improvements

    Results:
    • Print runs dramatically increased
    • Greater distribution networks
    • Intercontinental markets more linked

    Key Material Feature: SPEED

    Increased speed in production, distribution AND consumption of books. How did this impact translation?
    Bachleitner (2009): Demonstrated existence of "translation factories" as part of massive increase in printed production in German after 1820

    Impact: Upscaling contributed to:
    • Expansion of print access beyond learned elite
    • Greater emphasis on readability, comprehensibility, fluency (Bachleitner, 2016)
    Littau (2011): "Fast reading presupposes not only literacy but also a readable page and easily digestible prose" (p. 274)

    Link made: Between reading practices of time and translation strategies of fluency/readability

    Connection: Fluency, readability, intelligibility intimately connected to speed of production

    Book Series

    Characteristics:
    • Uniform editions
    • Similar formats
    • Widespread diffusion and popularity
    • Standardization

    Examples:
    • Tauchnitz (German firm) - Europe-wide distribution influenced Finnish translation practices (Paloposki, 2019)
    • Religious book series with stock images (O'Connor, 2017)

    Questions to ask:
    • Impact of homogeneity of material presentation on translation product?
    • Impact on translation practices?
    • How trajectories influenced by inclusion in book series vs standalone items?

    üì∞ 8. Periodicals - UNDEREXPLORED AREA

    Characteristics:
    • Incredibly prolific and varied
    • Central role in intellectual, literary, political history
    • Formation of modern communication/information systems
    • Mutable form - highly responsive to political, cultural, market forces
    • Ephemeral print product - designed for immediate use

    Diverse Modalities of Translation

    O'Connor (2019): Periodicals contain translation typologies NOT always evident in book trade:
    • Anonymous verse or prose translations
    • Insertions in review articles
    • Extracts
    • Segmented and serialized pieces across issues
    • Experimental approaches
    • Pseudotranslations
    • Unacknowledged borrowing
    • Creative reinterpretations and adaptations

    Impact of Medium

    Elements impacting translation types/forms:
    • Speed of publication process
    • Serialization of content
    • Immediacy and diffusion of medium
    • Dialogue between journals

    Mutable form allows for:
    • Expanded translational presences
    • Widened participation in translation enterprise

    Challenge: Ephemerality

    Two challenges:
    1. Fewer examples survive (ephemeral nature) ‚Üí part of "blank spaces" of translation history (Santoyo, 2006; van Doorslaer, 2011)
    2. Issue of VALUE: If media perceived as disposable/ephemeral, less likely to be valued and studied
    Important to study translations in disposable, ephemeral forms! Periodicals had potential to reach wider communities, engage differently than books. Cheap format ‚â† less important
    Colombo (2019a): Argued for adjustment in privileged focus on books - need to account for more ephemeral forms. Study of street literature for rounded picture of transnational book history

    Illustrate emerging complexity of modalities and materialities under translation studies umbrella
