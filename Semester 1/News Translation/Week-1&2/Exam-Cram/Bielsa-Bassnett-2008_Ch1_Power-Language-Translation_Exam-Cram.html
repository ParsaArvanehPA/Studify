Nida: Formal vs Dynamic Equivalence
Formal = word-for-word | Dynamic = sense-for-sense, broader view
Skopos Theory (Reiss & Vermeer, 1980s): Purpose determines translation method. Key = equivalent EFFECT not binary equivalence. Particularly useful for news translation.
Cultural Turn (Early 1990s): Translation NOT isolated - linked to world/context. Broadened focus from text to include source AND target contexts.
Even-Zohar (1990): Culture seeking renewal = MORE translation | Self-sufficient culture = LESS translation
