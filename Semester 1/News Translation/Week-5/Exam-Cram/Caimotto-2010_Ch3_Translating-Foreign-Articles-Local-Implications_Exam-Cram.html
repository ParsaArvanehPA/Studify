üî• CRITICAL LINGUISTIC ANALYSIS

‚úì Italian Adjective Position Rule (Serianni 2000)

BEFORE noun: Descriptive function (enriching description)

AFTER noun: Restrictive function (identifies/specifies)


Example:

  • "Le vecchie tubature" = The old pipes (all old ones)
  • "Le tubature vecchie" = The pipes that are old (only old ones, not new)

IN THE TRANSLATION:

  • Used: "La risicata vittoria" (descriptive - just describing quality)
  • Should be: "La vittoria risicata" (restrictive - identifying as narrow)

‚ö†Ô∏è RESULT: Made it seem problem is Prodi winning, NOT narrow margin!

Was it deliberate? Probably NOT - likely time pressure + source language influence. BUT still contributes to distortion!

üìå KEY TERMINOLOGY

TRANSLATION-REPORTING (term introduced by Caimotto):
Various ways of (re)presenting foreign articles, ranging from:
  • Translation proper (Jakobson 1959)
  • Transediting (Hursti 2001)
  • Reporting comments without quoting ST
INFOTAINMENT: Mixing of information and entertainment - instinct to amuse drives out will to report/analyze in depth (Hargreaves 2003)

üîç CAUSES OF MANIPULATION

‚úì 5 Main Causes

1. Time Pressure & Sloppiness

  • Key constraint but NOT single cause
  • Professional translators should work properly under pressure

2. Infotainment Culture

  • Stronger in Italy than other countries
  • Berlusconi's media control (1984 - broke laws, broadcast nationally)
  • Sensationalism over precision

3. Loporcaro's Framework (2005) - MEMORIZE!

Two Conceptions of News:

  • Traditional: News as INFORMATION - pivotal for democracy
  • Modern: News as NARRATION - offering stories like myths (Lule 2001)

In this case: FT article = new episode where centre-left attacked by British (like The Economist attacked Berlusconi before)

4. Historical: "Questione della Lingua"

  • Started 1500 - debate favoring classical Italian
  • Left population out of public discussion
  • 1970s-80s massification weakened quality margins

5. Translation as Extra Filter

  • Domestic issues: Different providers point out distortions
  • Foreign texts: Translation adds filter, no alternative versions
  • Few readers check source text
  • Lack of professional translator recognition

‚ö° QUICK FACTS

Italian Practice:
  • Common to report foreign articles about Italy
  • Translation process "hardly ever made visible"
  • Often done by journalists, NOT professional translators
  • Readers curious about foreign opinions (especially after The Economist 2001 articles against Berlusconi)
Translation Quality Issues in Corriere:
  • "Sensible" ‚Üí "sensibile" (wrong: should be different word; sensibile = sensitive)
  • "Default" used wrongly (non-technical Italian: only for computers; should be "insolvenza")
  • Too technical ‚Üí average reader stops after first paragraphs
  • Infers: FT against Prodi (like The Economist against Berlusconi)
Embedded Comments Functions (Corriere):
  1. Signal reporting someone else's words
  2. Distance from Munchau's opinion
  3. Guide readers (paragraph headings)
  4. Most important: Frame FT/Munchau authority positively
  5. Add reactions (EU Commission, etc.)
