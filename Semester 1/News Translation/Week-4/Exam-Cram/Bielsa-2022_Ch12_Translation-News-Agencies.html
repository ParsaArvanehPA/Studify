üéØ Core Concept

News agencies = B2B organizations (mostly) providing news gathering/distribution to media outlets. Were FIRST multinational corporations. Agence Havas (AFP precursor) specialized in translating foreign press.

Type Key Features Examples
Global Extensive bureaus worldwide; TV agencies AP, AFP, Reuters
(Xinhua growing)

üîë Agenda-Setting Power - CRITICAL

Undisputed Among Scholars: Newswires determine which topics become news. "Issues ignored by wholesalers seldom considered by other media" (Lorenz 2017: 949)
Evidence:
  • Paterson (2005): 43-60% of "original" content from AP/Reuters newsrooms
  • Lewis et al. (2008): Nearly half of 5 prestigious London newspapers from agencies
  • Welbers et al. (2018): Dutch media increased reliance 1996-2013

Churnalism

Definition (Davies 2009): Uncritical use of PR material (press releases)

Key finding: News agency can become "de facto distributor of public relations material" (Forde & Johnston 2013: 113)

üíª Internet Era - Know This

Why Agencies Still Thrive:
  • Diversification: Financial news, video, databases, IT solutions, weather, PR dissemination
  • State support: Direct/indirect (AFP, Xinhua)
  • Expanded client base: Newspapers/broadcasters have fewer local bureaus
  • Multimedia: Convergent newsrooms (Reuters, dpa, AAP)
B2B/B2C blurring: Some agencies (Reuters 2015) make content available to end users, BUT "many persist primarily as news wholesalers" (Boyd-Barrett 2016: 164)

Social Media

AP & AFP: Use social networks "first and foremost as tool for newsgathering" - cover hot-spots from which absent

‚öôÔ∏è Characteristics - EXAM CRITICAL

1. Journalistic Values (Priority Order)

1. ACCURACY - Unquestioned, first and most decisive
2. SPEED - Time pressure clearly acknowledged (Exception: IPS has more time)
3. COST-EFFECTIVENESS - Quest for efficiency
4. Audience tailoring - Important but not as strong (dispatches for journalists, not public)
5. Language quality - Matters but less than accuracy/speed

2. Journalists NOT Translators - THE RULE

General observation: Agencies do NOT hire translators (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009; Davier 2017)

Exceptions: IPS (alternative) has in-house translators; Efe Arabic service
"Natural/Non-professional translators": Bilingual individuals presumed able to translate without formal training
  • Not trained in translation
  • No specific guidelines
  • "Learning by doing" - don't think about practice

3. Lay Understanding of Translation - KEY PROBLEM

Problem: Journalists view translation as LEXICAL TRANSFER (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009; Davier 2014)

Consequences:
  • Conceived as literal = simple, no training needed
  • Don't acknowledge difficulties or seek help
  • Risks for accuracy
  • Translation = lesser importance (soft news, non-urgent, smaller audiences)

4. Contradictory Practices - EXAM ESSENTIAL

Practice Description
Invisibility In 344,850-word corpus: "translate" occurred 4 times only, never about dispatches (Davier 2017)
Avoidance "It's a job no one really likes doing" - make excuses to avoid it
Deselection Avoid quotations needing translation; "reselect" quotes in target language (faster, more tailored)
Major Rewritings Additions, deletions, paragraph reordering, title/lead changes
Literal Quotations Quotations translated word-for-word (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009; Scammell 2018)
PARADOX EXPLANATION: "One has to be extremely faithful with translation when dealing with quotations... and not confined to this precision when... explaining" (AFP senior journalist, Davier 2017: 144)

If translation = lexical transfer ‚Üí avoid it for rewriting when free, but use unidiomatic transfer when compelled by original's form (quotes)

5. Verbal Patchwork (Davier 2017: 208)

News item consists of:
  • Quotations (original language)
  • Translated quotations
  • Culture-specific concepts
  • Press release text (translated or not)
  • Translated explanations
  • Text written from scratch
  • Background from archive

üìê Consequences for Translation Studies - CRITICAL

Problems with Fundamental Terms

Translation in agencies "resists the definition of translation" (Bielsa & Bassnett 2009: 132)
Concept Problem Solution
Source Can be SEVERAL sources; uncertainty which was model Refer to "first"/"second" segment or language A/B
Target Can be SEVERAL targets Question usefulness of category
Author Collective authorship Revisit individual concept from literary field
Text Unit rarely complete dispatch; elements in quotes & culture-specific concepts Compare TEXT SEGMENTS, not full texts
Study translations WITHOUT originals

Domestication vs Foreignization

Bielsa & Bassnett (2009): "Dominant strategy = absolute domestication" (applies to texts/whole production)

Scammell (2018): Proposes foreignization at phrase/sentence level (introduce foreign words). Reader experiment: doesn't hurt readability (except in headlines)

Problem: They don't refer to same unit of translation!

Equivalence - Fails in News Agencies

Why it fails:
  • Linguistic equivalence: Major interventions + invisibility = "no identified source text"
  • Equivalent effect: Audiences diverge dramatically (AFP English Asia vs French France) = must respond to different needs = equivalence meaningless

New Concepts

Indirect Translation: Translation of a translation. Bridge concept between different TS branches - may overcome fragmentation (Assis Rosa et al. 2017: 113)

üîÆ Future Research Needed

  • Visual output (photos, infographics, videos) understudied
