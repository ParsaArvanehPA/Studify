Session 4: Surah Al-Fajr (The Dawn)

Verses 1-30: Divine Oaths, Historical Lessons, and the Tranquil Soul
From Ancient Civilizations to Eternal Peace

Introduction to Surah Al-Fajr

Overview

Name: Al-Fajr (الفجر) - "The Dawn"

Classification: Meccan Surah

Verses: 30 verses

Position: Chapter 89

Central Theme: Divine oaths establishing truth, lessons from destroyed civilizations, human misunderstanding of tests, and the ultimate peace of the tranquil soul

Verses 1-5: The Sacred Oaths

1-5 وَ الْفَجْرِ وَ لَيالٍ عَشْرٍ وَ الشَّفْعِ وَ الْوَتْرِ وَ اللَّيْلِ إِذا يَسْرِ هَلْ فِي ذلِكَ قَسَمٌ لِذِي حِجْرٍ

Translation:

"By the dawn. And by the ten nights. And by the even and the odd. And by the night when it passes. Is there in this oath sufficient assurance for those who have understanding?"

The Four Divine Oaths

OathArabicInterpretation
1. The DawnالْفَجْرِThe breaking of light after darkness; symbol of hope, renewal, truth emerging
2. Ten Nightsلَيالٍ عَشْرٍFirst ten nights of Dhul-Hijjah (including Day of Arafah) - most sacred time
3. Even & Oddالشَّفْعِ وَ الْوَتْرِCreation's duality (pairs) and Allah's unity (singularity)
4. Passing Nightاللَّيْلِ إِذا يَسْرِNight departing, giving way to dawn - cycle of life and death
ذِي حِجْرٍ (Dhī Hijr): "One who possesses understanding/intelligence" - those capable of reflection and wisdom. The oaths are sufficient proof for intelligent minds.

Verses 6-14: Lessons from Destroyed Nations

6-14 أَ لَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعادٍ إِرَمَ ذاتِ الْعِمادِ الَّتِي لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُها فِي الْبِلادِ وَ ثَمُودَ الَّذِينَ جابُوا الصَّخْرَ بِالْوادِ وَ فِرْعَوْنَ ذِي الْأَوْتادِ الَّذِينَ طَغَوْا فِي الْبِلادِ فَأَكْثَرُوا فِيهَا الْفَسادَ فَصَبَّ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّكَ سَوْطَ عَذابٍ إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصادِ

Translation:

"Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with 'Ad - Iram of the pillars, the likes of whom had never been created in the land? And Thamud, who carved out rocks in the valley? And Pharaoh, owner of the stakes? All of whom transgressed in the lands and spread much corruption therein. So your Lord poured upon them a scourge of punishment. Indeed, your Lord is ever watchful."

Three Civilizations of Pride and Destruction

لَبِالْمِرْصادِ (La-bil-Mirsād): "Ever watchful" - Allah is always observing, waiting. No oppressor escapes divine justice. A warning and a comfort.

Verses 15-20: The Human Misunderstanding of Tests

15-20 فَأَمَّا الْإِنْسانُ إِذا مَا ابْتَلاهُ رَبُّهُ فَأَكْرَمَهُ وَ نَعَّمَهُ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّي أَكْرَمَنِ وَ أَمَّا إِذا مَا ابْتَلاهُ فَقَدَرَ عَلَيْهِ رِزْقَهُ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّي أَهانَنِ كَلاَّ بَلْ لا تُكْرِمُونَ الْيَتِيمَ وَ لا تَحَاضُّونَ عَلى‏ طَعامِ الْمِسْكِينِ وَ تَأْكُلُونَ التُّراثَ أَكْلاً لَمًّا وَ تُحِبُّونَ الْمالَ حُبًّا جَمًّا

Translation:

"As for man, when his Lord tries him by honoring him and favoring him, he says: 'My Lord has honored me.' But when He tries him by restricting his provision, he says: 'My Lord has humiliated me.' No! Rather, you do not honor the orphan, and you do not encourage feeding the poor. And you consume inheritance, devouring it altogether. And you love wealth with immense love."

Critical Misunderstanding Corrected

Human Error: Equating wealth with divine favor and poverty with divine punishment

Divine Truth: Both wealth and poverty are TESTS (ابْتَلاهُ - trials)

  • Wealth Test: Will you be grateful? Will you share? Will you become arrogant?
  • Poverty Test: Will you remain patient? Will you trust Allah? Will you become bitter?

Four Social Crimes Exposed

  1. لا تُكْرِمُونَ الْيَتِيمَ: You don't honor the orphan (neglect vulnerable)
  2. لا تَحَاضُّونَ عَلى طَعامِ الْمِسْكِينِ: You don't encourage feeding the poor (social indifference)
  3. تَأْكُلُونَ التُّراثَ أَكْلاً لَمًّا: You devour inheritance greedily (financial exploitation)
  4. تُحِبُّونَ الْمالَ حُبًّا جَمًّا: You love wealth with excessive love (materialism)

Verses 21-26: The Day of Judgment

21-26 كَلاَّا إِذا دُكَّتِ الْأَرْضُ دَكًّا دَكًّا وَ جاءَ رَبُّكَ وَ الْمَلَكُ صَفًّا صَفًّا وَ جِي‏ءَ يَوْمَئِذٍ بِجَهَنَّمَ يَوْمَئِذٍ يَتَذَكَّرُ الْإِنْسانُ وَ أَنَّى لَهُ الذِّكْرى‏ يَقُولُ يا لَيْتَنِي قَدَّمْتُ لِحَياتِي فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لا يُعَذِّبُ عَذابَهُ أَحَدٌ وَ لا يُوثِقُ وَثاقَهُ أَحَدٌ

Translation:

"No! When the earth is leveled, pounded and crushed. And your Lord comes with the angels, rank upon rank. And Hell is brought forth that Day - on that Day man will remember, but what good will remembrance be to him? He will say: 'Oh, I wish I had sent ahead for my life!' For on that Day, none will punish as He punishes. And none will bind as He binds."

دُكَّتِ الْأَرْضُ دَكًّا دَكًّا (Dukkat al-Ard Dakkan Dakkan): "Earth is pounded/leveled thoroughly" - repetition emphasizes complete devastation. Mountains leveled, earth flattened - end of worldly geography.

The Ultimate Regret

يا لَيْتَنِي قَدَّمْتُ لِحَياتِي - "Oh, I wish I had sent ahead for my LIFE!"

The painful realization: THIS (afterlife) is the real life. The worldly life was merely preparation. But the realization comes too late.

Verses 27-30: The Tranquil Soul

27-30 يا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلى‏ رَبِّكِ راضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبادِي وَ ادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي

Translation:

"O tranquil soul! Return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing [to Him]. Enter among My [righteous] servants. And enter My Paradise."

The Tranquil Soul (النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ)

Al-Nafs al-Mutma'innah: The soul at peace - highest spiritual station

Four Stages of the Soul in Quran:

TypeDescriptionReference
النَّفْسُ الأَمَّارَةCommanding soul - incites to evilSurah Yusuf 12:53
النَّفْسُ اللَّوَّامَةSelf-reproaching soul - conscience awakenedSurah Al-Qiyamah 75:2

The Divine Call: Four Commands

  1. ارْجِعِي إِلى رَبِّكِ: Return to your Lord
  2. راضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً: Well-pleased and pleasing to Him
  3. فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبادِي: Enter among My servants
  4. وَ ادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي: And enter My Paradise

Note: "My Paradise" (جَنَّتِي) - personal, intimate address from Allah. Not "a" paradise, but "MY" paradise.

Contemporary Application

Lessons for Modern Life

  • Beyond Materialism: Wealth and poverty are both tests, not indicators of worth
  • Social Responsibility: Care for orphans, feed the poor, encourage charity
  • Historical Awareness: Learn from destroyed civilizations - arrogance leads to ruin
  • Inner Peace: Cultivate tranquil soul through submission to Allah
  • True Life: Prepare for the real life (afterlife), not just this temporary existence
"O tranquil soul! Return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing to Him" - The ultimate destination of peace.
