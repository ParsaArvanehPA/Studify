Session 6: Surah Al-Buruj (The Constellations)

Verses 1-22: The Story of Martyrdom and Divine Justice
From the People of the Trench to the Preserved Tablet

Introduction to Surah Al-Buruj

Overview

Name: Al-Buruj (البروج) - "The Constellations" or "The Great Stars"

Classification: Meccan Surah

Verses: 22 verses

Position: Chapter 85

Central Theme: Divine oaths witnessing truth, story of martyrs burned in trenches, warnings to persecutors, promise of Paradise for believers, and the Quran's preservation in the Guarded Tablet

Verses 1-3: The Three Oaths

1-3 وَ السَّماءِ ذاتِ الْبُرُوجِ وَ الْيَوْمِ الْمَوْعُودِ وَ شاهِدٍ وَ مَشْهُودٍ

Translation:

"By the sky containing great constellations. And by the promised Day. And by the witness and what is witnessed."

The Three Divine Oaths

OathArabicInterpretation
1. Sky with Constellations السَّماءِ ذاتِ الْبُرُوجِ The magnificent celestial order - Allah's creative power and eternal watchfulness
2. The Promised Day الْيَوْمِ الْمَوْعُودِ Day of Judgment - when all will be held accountable
3. Witness & Witnessed شاهِدٍ وَ مَشْهُودٍ Multiple interpretations:
- Friday & Day of Arafah
- Angels witnessing humans
- Prophet witnessing his community
- All creation witnessing events
البُرُوج (Al-Buruj): The great stars/constellations - literally "towers" or "fortresses" in the sky. Refers to the zodiac constellations through which the sun, moon, and planets travel. Symbol of cosmic order and divine design.

Verses 4-9: The Story of the People of the Trench

4-9 قُتِلَ أَصْحابُ الْأُخْدُودِ النَّارِ ذاتِ الْوَقُودِ إِذْ هُمْ عَلَيْها قُعُودٌ وَ هُمْ عَلى‏ ما يَفْعَلُونَ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ شُهُودٌ وَ ما نَقَمُوا مِنْهُمْ إِلاَّ أَنْ يُؤْمِنُوا بِاللَّـهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّماواتِ وَ الْأَرْضِ وَ اللَّـهُ عَلى‏ كُلِّ شَيْ‏ءٍ شَهِيدٌ

Translation:

"Cursed were the makers of the trench - the fire of abundant fuel - when they were sitting by it. And they witnessed what they were doing to the believers. And they resented them not except because they believed in Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Praiseworthy - to whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth. And Allah, over all things, is Witness."

Historical Context: The People of the Trench (Ashab al-Ukhdud)

What Happened:

  • A tyrannical king (possibly in Najran, Yemen, or ancient Himyar) dug trenches
  • Filled them with fire and burning fuel
  • Gave believers an ultimatum: renounce faith or be thrown into fire
  • Believers chose death over apostasy
  • Thousands were burned alive while persecutors sat watching

Historical Accounts:

  • Najran Story (520 CE): Christian community persecuted by Jewish Himyarite king Dhu Nuwas
  • Some scholars: Could refer to multiple such incidents throughout history
  • Common element: Believers murdered solely for their faith

The Famous Story of the Boy: One narration mentions a young believer who encouraged his mother to jump into the fire, saying the temporary pain is nothing compared to eternal hellfire. The baby miraculously spoke, strengthening the mother's resolve.

Their Only "Crime"

وَ ما نَقَمُوا مِنْهُمْ إِلاَّ أَنْ يُؤْمِنُوا بِاللَّـهِ: "And they resented them not except for their belief in Allah"

The persecutors had NO legitimate grievance. The believers didn't harm anyone, didn't steal, didn't rebel - they simply believed. This exposes the pure injustice and irrationality of persecution for faith.

الْأُخْدُود (Al-Ukhdūd): The trench/pit - a deep ditch dug in the ground. الْوَقُود (Al-Waqūd): The fuel - materials used to intensify the fire (wood, oil, etc.). The detailed description emphasizes the horrific nature of the torture.

Verses 10-11: Warning and Promise

10-11 إِنَّ الَّذِينَ فَتَنُوا الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَ الْمُؤْمِناتِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَتُوبُوا فَلَهُمْ عَذابُ جَهَنَّمَ وَ لَهُمْ عَذابُ الْحَرِيقِ إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَ عَمِلُوا الصَّالِحاتِ لَهُمْ جَنَّاتٌ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهارُ ذلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْكَبِيرُ

Translation:

"Indeed, those who have tortured the believing men and believing women and then have not repented will have the punishment of Hell, and they will have the punishment of the Burning Fire. Indeed, those who have believed and done righteous deeds will have gardens beneath which rivers flow. That is the great attainment."

The Door of Repentance

ثُمَّ لَمْ يَتُوبُوا: "Then have not repented"

Even for such heinous crimes, the door of repentance remains open until death. Allah's mercy is so vast that even those who burned believers alive can be forgiven if they sincerely repent. Some scholars note this as one of the most merciful verses - showing no sin is beyond Allah's forgiveness if one truly turns back.

Verses 12-16: Divine Attributes

12-16 إِنَّ بَطْشَ رَبِّكَ لَشَدِيدٌ إِنَّهُ هُوَ يُبْدِئُ وَ يُعِيدُ وَ هُوَ الْغَفُورُ الْوَدُودُ ذُو الْعَرْشِ الْمَجِيدُ فَعَّالٌ لِما يُرِيدُ

Translation:

"Indeed, the vengeance of your Lord is severe. Indeed, it is He who originates and repeats. And He is the Forgiving, the Affectionate - Owner of the Glorious Throne - Effecter of what He intends."

الْوَدُودُ (Al-Wadūd): The Affectionate/Loving - one of Allah's beautiful names. After mentioning severe punishment, Allah mentions His love. Balance of justice and mercy. The same Lord who punishes oppressors deeply loves His believing servants.

Verses 17-20: Historical Examples

17-20 هَلْ أَتاكَ حَدِيثُ الْجُنُودِ فِرْعَوْنَ وَ ثَمُودَ بَلِ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فِي تَكْذِيبٍ وَ اللَّـهُ مِنْ وَرائِهِمْ مُحِيطٌ

Translation:

"Has there come to you the story of the legions - Pharaoh and Thamud? But those who disbelieve are in denial. And Allah is, from behind them, encompassing."

Pharaoh and Thamud: Parallel Stories

  • Pharaoh: Persecuted believers (Israelites), claimed divinity, drowned in Red Sea
  • Thamud: Advanced civilization, rejected Prophet Salih, destroyed by earthquake
  • Common Thread: Power, arrogance, persecution, divine destruction
  • Message: The makers of the trench are following the path of destroyed tyrants

Allah's Encompassing Knowledge

وَ اللَّـهُ مِنْ وَرائِهِمْ مُحِيطٌ: "And Allah is, from behind them, encompassing"

Allah surrounds them with His knowledge and power. They think they're in control, but Allah encompasses them completely. No matter how powerful persecutors seem, they cannot escape divine justice.

Verses 21-22: The Preserved Quran

21-22 بَلْ هُوَ قُرْآنٌ مَجِيدٌ فِي لَوْحٍ مَحْفُوظٍ

Translation:

"But this is an honored Quran. In a Preserved Tablet."

The Quran's Divine Protection

قُرْآنٌ مَجِيدٌ: "Honored/Glorious Quran" - elevated in status and nobility

لَوْحٍ مَحْفُوظٍ: "Preserved Tablet" (Al-Lawh al-Mahfūdh)

Significance:

  • Pre-existence: Quran existed with Allah before creation
  • Divine protection: Cannot be corrupted or changed
  • Eternal truth: Message is permanent and unchanging
  • Contrast: Just as believers' martyrdom is recorded and remembered, the Quran itself is preserved eternally

Connection to the Surah's Theme

The Surah begins with cosmic order (constellations), moves through human persecution, and ends with divine preservation. Message: Just as the stars remain in their ordained positions and the Quran remains preserved despite enemies, so too will the truth and the believers ultimately triumph, no matter the temporary persecution.

Contemporary Application

Lessons for Modern Life

  • Steadfastness in Faith: Believers chose death over apostasy - faith is worth any sacrifice
  • Persecution is Not New: Believers throughout history faced torture; we must remember and learn
  • Justice Delayed, Not Denied: Oppressors may seem to win temporarily, but divine justice is certain
  • Door of Repentance: Even heinous criminals can repent - mercy is always available
  • Divine Encompassment: Allah surrounds all with His knowledge - no evil escapes
  • Quran's Preservation: The message has survived 1400+ years unchanged - proof of divine protection
"And they resented them not except because they believed in Allah" - Pure faith, though it costs everything, is worth everything.
