Session 3: Surah Al-Insan (The Human)

Verses 1-22: The Journey from Nothingness to Paradise
Gratitude, Charity, and Divine Reward

Introduction to Surah Al-Insan

Overview

Name: Al-Insan (الإنسان) - "The Human" | Also called Ad-Dahr (الدهر) - "Time"

Classification: Medinan Surah

Verses: 31 verses (we study 1-22)

Position: Chapter 76

Central Theme: Human origin, purpose, moral choices, and ultimate reward or punishment

Verses 1-3: Human Origins and Free Will

1-3 هَلْ أَتى‏ عَلَى الْإِنْسانِ حِينٌ مِنَ الدَّهْرِ لَمْ يَكُنْ شَيْئاً مَذْكُوراً ‏ إِنَّا خَلَقْنَا الْإِنْسانَ مِنْ نُطْفَةٍ أَمْشاجٍ نَبْتَلِيهِ فَجَعَلْناهُ سَمِيعاً بَصِيراً إِنَّا هَدَيْناهُ السَّبِيلَ إِمَّا شاكِراً وَ إِمَّا كَفُوراً

Translation:

"Has there come upon man a period of time when he was not a thing worth mentioning? Indeed, We created man from a sperm-drop mixture that We may try him; and We made him hearing and seeing. Indeed, We guided him to the way, be he grateful or be he ungrateful."

نُطْفَةٍ أَمْشاجٍ (Nutfatin Amshāj): "Mixed sperm-drop" - scientifically accurate reference to genetic material from both parents, mentioned 1400 years ago!

The Fundamental Choice

إِمَّا شاكِراً وَ إِمَّا كَفُوراً: "Either grateful or ungrateful"

This binary choice defines human existence. After being given faculties (hearing, seeing) and guidance, humans must choose:

  • شاكِر (Shākir): Grateful - recognizes blessings and worships Allah
  • كَفُور (Kafūr): Ungrateful/Denier - rejects truth despite clear evidence

Verses 4-6: Paradise for the Righteous

Rewards of the Grateful

Verse 5: "Indeed, the righteous will drink from a cup of wine whose mixture is of Kafur (camphor)"

Verse 6: "A spring from which the servants of Allah will drink; they will make it gush forth in force"

Kafur (كافُور): A cool, fragrant substance - symbolizing refreshing, pure pleasure (opposite of worldly intoxicants)

Verses 7-10: The Character of the Righteous

7-10 يُوفُونَ بِالنَّذْرِ وَ يَخافُونَ يَوْماً كانَ شَرُّهُ مُسْتَطِيراً وَ يُطْعِمُونَ الطَّعامَ عَلى‏ حُبِّهِ مِسْكِيناً وَ يَتِيماً وَ أَسِيراً إِنَّما نُطْعِمُكُمْ لِوَجْهِ اللَّـهِ لا نُرِيدُ مِنْكُمْ جَزاءً وَ لا شُكُوراً إِنَّا نَخافُ مِنْ رَبِّنا يَوْماً عَبُوساً قَمْطَرِيراً

Translation:

"They fulfill vows and fear a Day whose evil will be widespread. And they give food despite love for it to the needy, the orphan, and the captive, [Saying], 'We feed you only for the countenance of Allah. We wish not from you reward or gratitude. Indeed, we fear from our Lord a Day austere and distressful.'"

Historical Context: The Story of Ahlul-Bayt

Classical scholars narrate that these verses were revealed about the family of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (some say Ali, Fatimah, and their sons). They had very little food but gave it to a needy person, an orphan, and a captive on three consecutive nights, breaking their fast with only water.

The Lesson: True charity is giving what you yourself need and love, purely for Allah's sake, expecting nothing in return.

عَبُوساً قَمْطَرِيراً ('Abūsan Qamtarīran): "Austere and distressful" - describes the Day of Judgment. عَبُوس = frowning, severe; قَمْطَرِير = extremely difficult. The intense Arabic emphasizes the terror of that Day.

سَلْسَبِيلاً (Salsabīlan): Name of a spring in Paradise. From سَلْسَل (flowing smoothly). The beauty of the name itself evokes the pleasure of drinking from it - easy, refreshing, delightful.

Contemporary Application

Lessons for Modern Life

  • Sincere Charity: Give what you love, expect nothing in return
  • Humility in Giving: Don't seek recognition or praise
  • Fear & Hope: Balance fear of Judgment with hope in Paradise
  • Scientific Accuracy: Quran's embryology description validates divine origin
"We feed you only for the countenance of Allah" - Pure sincerity is the mark of true faith.
